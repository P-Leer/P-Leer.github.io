01 / Research

Small models.
Human-scale problems.

My work combines signal processing, perceptual models, and data-driven methods to investigate audio systems that are useful outside the lab.

01

Speech enhancement

Adaptive noise-reduction systems that exploit context while respecting the computational limits of hearing devices.

02

Auditory modelling

Efficient neural emulators of the auditory pathway, designed to retain behaviour across frequency, level, and hearing loss.

03

Contextual adaptation

Understanding which acoustic and listener-specific cues allow compact models to specialise effectively.

03 / About

PL

Research should survive contact with the real world.

I am a scientist at Eriksholm Research Centre, working on artificial intelligence for speech enhancement and hearing technology.

My research asks how detailed knowledge of hearing, acoustic context, and individual listeners can lead to better signal-processing systems. I am particularly interested in methods that remain effective when compute, data, and latency are constrained.
