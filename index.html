Current work

Context-aware neural speech enhancement

Hearing devices operate with tight limits on memory, computation, and latency. Instead of asking one large model to handle every situation, I study whether compact models can specialise to the context at hand.

The central question is not simply whether adaptation helps, but which information is worth adapting to. Speaker identity, language, noise, and the acoustic scene carry different amounts of useful information. Understanding that ranking can guide systems that learn efficiently from everyday listening situations.
